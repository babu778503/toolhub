General Leather Cleaning Steps

  1. Assess the leather type and mark severity before cleaning
  2. Remove surface dirt with a soft cloth or brush
  3. Apply the recommended cleaning solution according to the instructions below
  4. Allow the solution to sit for the recommended time
  5. Buff with a clean, dry cloth
  6. Apply leather conditioner if needed

Recommended Leather Care Products

  • For scratches: Leather filler or scratch remover
  • For claw marks: Leather repair kit
  • For chewing: Leather restoration cream
  • For stains: Saddle soap or leather cleaner

Helpful Tips

  • Always test cleaning solutions on a hidden area first
  • Work in a well-ventilated area
  • Use only leather-specific cleaners and conditioners
  • For deep scratches, consider professional restoration
  • Regular conditioning prevents cracking and wear

Leather Care

Each leather type requires specific care. Aniline leather needs gentle products, while pigmented leather is more durable.

Prevention

Regular maintenance and immediate attention to marks can prevent permanent damage and extend leather life.

Professional Help

For valuable or antique leather items, consider professional restoration services.
