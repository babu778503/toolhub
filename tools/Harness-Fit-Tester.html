Harness Fit Information

Proper harness fit ensures your pet's comfort and safety:

  • Measure chest at the widest point behind front legs
  • Measure neck where collar normally sits
  • Allow two fingers between harness and body
  • Check fit regularly as pets grow or change weight
Important: This tester provides size recommendations only. Always try actual harnesses on your pet. A properly fitted harness should not restrict movement or cause chafing. Too tight causes discomfort; too loose may allow escape.

Vest harnesses offer the most coverage and control. Step-in harnesses are easier to put on. Over-head harnesses may be quicker but can be stressful for some pets. No-pull harnesses help with leash training. Consider your pet's build, activity level, and any mobility issues when selecting a harness.
