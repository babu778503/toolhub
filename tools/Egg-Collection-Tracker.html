Egg Collection Information

Regular egg collection is important for maintaining quality and encouraging continued laying. Factors affecting production include:

  • Hen age (peaks at 6-18 months)
  • Laying rate (varies by breed)
  • Seasonal changes (light exposure)
  • Health and nutrition
Important: This tracker provides estimates for egg production. Actual collection may vary based on breed, health, stress, and environmental factors. Collect eggs daily to maintain freshness and prevent breakage.

Most hens lay eggs in the morning, so collect them before noon. Store eggs with the pointed end down in a cool place. Fresh eggs can be stored for 3-5 weeks at room temperature or 4-5 weeks in the refrigerator.
