General Testing Steps

  1. Visually inspect the entire fence for signs of damage or wear
  2. Check fence posts for stability by applying gentle pressure
  3. Examine fence panels for loose boards, sagging, or breaks
  4. Look for rust, corrosion, or other structural issues
  5. Ensure proper drainage around fence posts
  6. Check for loose or missing components

Recommended Maintenance

  • Apply wood preservatives for wooden fences
  • Tighten loose hardware regularly
  • Replace damaged sections promptly
  • Clear vegetation around the fence base
  • Reinforce weak posts if necessary

Helpful Tips

  • Test fence strength during dry conditions
  • Start testing from the most exposed areas
  • Document any issues found during testing
  • Address minor issues before they become major problems
  • Consider professional inspection for older fences

Safety First

Always wear appropriate safety gear when testing fence strength and be cautious of potential hazards.

Prevention

Regular maintenance prevents minor issues from becoming major safety hazards.

Professional Help

For structural concerns, always consult with a professional contractor.
