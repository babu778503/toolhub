Eligibility for compensation depends on various factors including airline, route, delay duration, and reason.

Remember to keep your boarding pass and flight documentation for any compensation claim.

Compensation Guidelines:

  • EU261 regulation applies to flights departing from EU airports or operated by EU airlines
  • Compensation amounts vary based on flight distance and delay duration
  • Delays under 3 hours typically do not qualify for compensation under EU261
  • Compensation may not be available if delay was caused by extraordinary circumstances
  • Claims must typically be filed within 2 years of the flight
  • First/Business class passengers may receive higher compensation

Regulation Information:

EU261/2004 - European regulation providing compensation for flight delays, cancellations, and denied boarding.

US DOT - Department of Transportation rules for flight delays and cancellations in the US.

Montreal Convention - International treaty covering airline liability for delays.
