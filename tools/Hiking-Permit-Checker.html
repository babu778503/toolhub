Permit Requirements for Your Hike

üìã Common Permit Types

  • Day Use Permit: Required for some popular day hiking areas
  • Backcountry Permit: Needed for overnight camping in wilderness areas
  • Wilderness Permit: Required for designated wilderness areas
  • Trail-Specific Permit: Some trails require special permits (e.g., Half Dome, Angels Landing)
  • Group Permit: Required for groups of 10+ people
  • Seasonal Permit: Required during peak seasons or special events
Pro Tip: Always check permit requirements at least 2-4 weeks before your planned hike, as some permits have limited availability and require advance reservations.

üéØ How to Obtain Permits

  • Visit the official website of the park or forest service managing your destination
  • Create an account on recreation.gov or the park's reservation system
  • Check availability for your dates and submit your application
  • Pay required fees (typically $5-30 per person per day)
  • Receive your permit via email and print it or save it on your mobile device
  • Carry your permit with you during your hike and present it if requested by rangers
Important: Permits are non-transferable and only valid for the specific dates and group size indicated. Violating permit conditions can result in fines and entry bans.
