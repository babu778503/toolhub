Soreness Scale Interpretation

  • None (0): No muscle soreness or discomfort
  • Mild (1-3): Slight soreness, noticeable only with movement
  • Moderate (4-5): Noticeable soreness during daily activities
  • Severe (6-7): Significant soreness affecting movement and comfort
  • Extreme (8-10): Severe pain limiting normal activities

Recovery Resources

Understanding Muscle Soreness

Acute Soreness: Occurs during or immediately after exercise due to muscle fatigue

Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS): Appears 24-72 hours after exercise due to microscopic muscle damage

Overuse Injury: Persistent pain that worsens with activity and doesn't improve with rest

Recovery Methods

  • Active Recovery: Light movement to increase blood flow
  • Stretching: Improves flexibility and reduces tension
  • Foam Rolling: Self-massage to release muscle tension
  • Hydration: Essential for nutrient transport and waste removal
  • Sleep: Critical for muscle repair and growth
  • Nutrition: Adequate protein and anti-inflammatory foods

When to Seek Help

  • Persistent pain that worsens over time
  • Sharp or shooting pain during movement
  • Swelling, redness, or warmth in the affected area
  • Pain that interferes with daily activities for more than a few days
  • Significant decrease in range of motion
