Technical Specifications

Waterproof Ratings Explained +

Waterproof ratings are measured in millimeters (mm) of water column. 5,000mm+ is suitable for light rain, 10,000mm+ for heavy rain, and 20,000mm+ for extreme conditions.

Breathability Ratings +

Breathability is measured in grams per square meter per day (g/m²/24hr). Higher values mean better moisture vapor transmission. Look for 5,000g+ for moderate activity, 10,000g+ for high activity.

Insulation Types +

Synthetic insulation retains warmth when wet and dries quickly. Down insulation provides superior warmth-to-weight ratio but loses effectiveness when wet.

Gear Care Instructions

💧 Weather Condition Guide

🌧️

Rain Protection

Look for gear with high waterproof ratings (measured in mm). A rating of 5,000mm+ is suitable for light rain, while 10,000mm+ is better for heavy downpours.

💨

Wind Protection

Choose tightly woven fabrics or materials with windproof membranes. Layering with a windproof outer shell is often the most effective approach.

❄️

Cold Weather Gear

Layering is key in cold conditions. Start with moisture-wicking base layers, add insulating mid-layers, and finish with a weatherproof shell.

🔥

Hot Weather Gear

Opt for lightweight, breathable fabrics with UV protection. Light colors reflect heat, and loose fits allow air circulation.

🎯 Gear Selection Tips

  • Always check the waterproof rating (measured in mm) - higher numbers offer better protection
  • Look for sealed seams and storm flaps on zippers for maximum weather protection
  • Consider breathability ratings - important for high-exertion activities
  • Ensure proper fit - too tight restricts movement, too loose reduces effectiveness
  • Invest in quality - better gear lasts longer and performs better in harsh conditions
  • Layering systems offer the most versatility - add or remove layers as conditions change
Pro Tip: Layering systems offer the most versatility. You can add or remove layers based on changing conditions.
