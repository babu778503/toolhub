Understanding Cholesterol

Cholesterol is a waxy, fat-like substance found in all cells of the body. Your body needs some cholesterol to make hormones, vitamin D, and substances that help you digest foods. Your body makes all the cholesterol it needs.

LDL (low-density lipoprotein) cholesterol is sometimes called "bad" cholesterol because high LDL cholesterol leads to a buildup of cholesterol in your arteries.

HDL (high-density lipoprotein) cholesterol is sometimes called "good" cholesterol because it carries cholesterol from other parts of your body back to your liver. Your liver then removes the cholesterol from your body.

Heart-Healthy Foods

  • Oily fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) - rich in omega-3 fatty acids
  • Nuts and seeds - almonds, walnuts, flaxseeds
  • Fruits and vegetables - berries, leafy greens, citrus fruits
  • Whole grains - oats, quinoa, brown rice
  • Legumes - beans, lentils, chickpeas
  • Avocados - rich in monounsaturated fats

Exercise Recommendations

  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week
  • Include muscle-strengthening activities on 2 or more days per week
  • Try brisk walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing
  • Start slowly and gradually increase intensity and duration
  • Find activities you enjoy to maintain consistency
